Marta Wambaugh

About

Marta Wambaugh is a creative and hardworking individual currently attending University of Washington’s Website Development Program. Marta spent her childhood in Seattle and is grateful to have lived in such a beautiful environment. After graduating Western Washington’s Woodring College of Education, Marta served as a public-school teacher in Seattle Schools for six years, also contributing her work with a local Montessori preschool and a teaching internship in New Zealand’s Havelock North High School Art Department. Marta has been writing detailed, trauma-responsive and inclusive Visual Art, Daily Living, and Health curriculum for almost a decade. She has thrived in high-paced workplaces and adjusts well to change and growth in her programs. When Marta isn’t putting in the extra hours at school and work: she enjoys her time outside gardening, hiking, bike-touring, and kayaking. Marta is a talented multi-media artist specializing in digital photography, fibers, and watercolors. Marta specializes in portraits and botanical photography. Her most impressive fiber projects continue to be her realistic reproductions of local food items for her annual Halloween costume. In less-than-ideal weather conditions Marta is often found inside enjoying cooking, hosting bingo night, and sewing for her friends and family.
